The Problem

The spreadsheet isn't the problem.
The assumptions feeding it are.

Most STR investors build their own pro formas. They pull a few comps off Airbnb, guess at an occupancy rate, and assume their nightly rate lands somewhere in the middle of what they see listed. Then they close.

Six months later, the property is underperforming. Not because the market is bad. Because the analysis didn't account for seasonal demand curves, channel mix realities, ramp-up timelines, or what comps actually earn versus what they list for.

There's a gap between what a property looks like it will earn and what it will actually earn once it's live on the OTAs. Closing that gap before you buy — that's what this service is built for.

Most STR pro formas are built by people who need the deal to close. Not by operators who have to live with the numbers.

  • Seller projections cherry-pick peak months and ignore ramp-up
  • Wrong comp sets inflate ADR assumptions from the start
  • Platform fee changes from 2025 make old models structurally wrong
  • Regulatory risk goes unresearched until it's too late
  • One scenario — the best case — drives a $300K decision
